Cameron Set to Return with U.K. Majority as SNP Sweeps Scotland

David Cameron is poised to return as U.K. prime minsiter after steering his Conservatives to an unexpected majority, helped by an landslide for nationalists in Scotland at Labour's expense.

The pound jumbed as projections of the final tally in the British general election indicated that the Conservatives had defined opinion polls to easily defeat Ed Miliband's Labout Party. With most seats declared, the BBC forecast the Tories to take 331 of Parliament's 650 seats to Labour's 232 seats, a result that would allow Cameron to ditch his Liberal Democrat coalition partnet of the past five years and govern alone.

With Cameron immediately renewing his pledge to hold a referndum on British membership of the European Union, the result throws up questions about Britain's constitutional future. For now, the surprise victory was welcomed by markets, with stocks and the pound rallying as it became clear Cameron had defied forecasts of a hung pariliament to easily defeat Ed Miliband's Labour Party and govern alone.


Buckingham Palace

The premier was meeting with Queen Elizabeth II at Buckingham Palace as the Conservatives reached the 326-seat threshold that allows them to ditch their Liberal Democrat coalition partners and govern alone in the 650-seat Parliament.
